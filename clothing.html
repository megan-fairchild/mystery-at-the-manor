The Wardrobe

Dress the part - or just dress comfortably. We don't judge at the manor.

Character Color Guide

Pick a Clue character and wear their signature color. Costumes are encouraged but not required - even just a solid-color shirt counts!

Miss Scarlet - Red dress, red top, or anything bold and crimson. Think glamorous and dramatic.
Colonel Mustard - Yellow or mustard tones. A blazer, polo, or button-down in warm gold.
Mrs. Peacock - Royal blue or teal. Elegant, put-together. A nice blouse or collared shirt.
Professor Plum - Purple, plum, or lavender. Scholarly vibes - maybe a vest or bow tie.
Mr. Green - Green - forest, emerald, or olive. Nervous energy optional.
Mrs. White - White or cream. Classic, composed, housekeeper chic. (Host is wearing a Mrs. White face shirt!)

Weather-Smart Layers

It will be warm when you arrive (~74F) but cool off quickly after sunset (~55F). Plan for both.

Arrival (6:00 PM): Comfortable warm-weather clothing. Shorts/skirt/dress or light pants. Your character color top.
Post-Sunset (8:00+ PM): Bring a jacket, hoodie, or flannel. Temperature drops fast once the sun is down. Jeans or long pants recommended for the movie.
Footwear: Closed-toe shoes or sneakers recommended (grass + dusk + running around for clues). Skip the heels unless you're truly committed to Miss Scarlet.
Blanket: Bring your own cozy blanket for the movie! The ground gets cool after dark.

For the Kids

6-year-old (Lead Detective): Comfortable play clothes in a character color. A detective hat or magnifying glass prop will be provided. Layer with a hoodie for the movie.
1-year-old: Comfy onesie or play outfit. Pack an extra layer and a blanket for when it cools down. PJs for the ride home.
