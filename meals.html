🍸 The Conservatory Cocktails

Miss Scarlet's Strawberry Lemonade

A bold, crimson refreshment - as daring as its namesake

Ingredients

  • 1 cup fresh strawberries, hulled and halved
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup fresh lemon juice (about 6 lemons)
  • 6 cups cold water
  • Ice and fresh strawberry slices for garnish

Directions

  1. Blend strawberries with sugar until smooth.
  2. Strain through a fine mesh sieve into a large pitcher.
  3. Add lemon juice and cold water. Stir well.
  4. Serve over ice, garnished with strawberry slices.

Professor Plum's Purple Punch

Mysterious, deep, and dangerously drinkable

Ingredients

  • 2 liters grape juice
  • 1 liter lemon-lime soda
  • 1 cup pineapple juice
  • Fresh grapes and lemon slices for garnish
  • Optional: frozen grape "ice cubes"

Directions

  1. Freeze grapes overnight for ice cubes that don't dilute.
  2. Combine grape juice and pineapple juice in a punch bowl.
  3. Add lemon-lime soda just before serving.
  4. Float frozen grapes and lemon slices on top.

🍲 First Course - "Dinner Is Served"

"Will you all be seated? Dinner is served." - Wadsworth

"I Can Assure You, It's Not Poisoned" Rolls

"I can assure you, the food is not poisoned!" - Wadsworth (sweating slightly)

Ingredients

  • 1 package (12 count) Hawaiian sweet rolls, or your favorite dinner rolls
  • 4 tbsp butter, melted
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • Pinch of flaky sea salt

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350F.
  2. Arrange rolls on a baking sheet. Brush tops with melted garlic butter.
  3. Bake 5-7 minutes until warm and golden on top.
  4. Sprinkle with parsley and flaky salt. Serve warm.
  5. Dramatically announce "I can assure you, they are NOT poisoned!" as you set them on the table.

"Mrs. Peacock's Favorite" - Creamy Tomato Bisque

"This soup's delicious, isn't it?" - Mrs. Peacock (eating with suspicious enthusiasm)

A rich, velvety tomato soup that Mrs. Peacock would absolutely devour. Optional addition to the first course.

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (28 oz each) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil for garnish

Directions

  1. Melt butter in a large pot. Saute onion until soft (5 min). Add garlic, cook 1 minute.
  2. Add crushed tomatoes, broth, sugar, and dried basil. Simmer 20 minutes.
  3. Blend with an immersion blender until smooth (or use a regular blender in batches).
  4. Stir in heavy cream. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Serve in small cups or bowls with a swirl of cream and fresh basil on top.

Make ahead: Can be made the day before and reheated. Even better the next day.

Main Course

"Mr. Boddy" Smoked Ribs

Slow-smoked, fall-off-the-bone ribs. The victim never tasted so good.

Ingredients

  • 4-5 racks baby back ribs (about 1 rack per 3 guests)
  • 1/4 cup paprika
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp onion powder
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Your favorite BBQ sauce
  • Apple juice for spritzing

Directions

  1. Remove membrane from back of ribs.
  2. Mix all dry spices into a rub. Coat ribs generously on all sides.
  3. Preheat electric smoker to 225F with your preferred wood chips (hickory or apple work great).
  4. Smoke ribs for 3 hours, spritzing with apple juice every 45 minutes.
  5. Wrap in foil with a splash of apple juice. Return to smoker for 2 hours.
  6. Unwrap, brush with BBQ sauce, and smoke 30 more minutes uncovered.
  7. Rest 10 minutes, slice, and serve.

Timing: Start smoker by noon for 6:30 PM dinner. Total cook time ~5.5 hours plus rest.

"Colonel Mustard's" Pulled Chicken

Tangy honey-mustard pulled chicken - the Colonel's secret recipe

Ingredients

  • 4 lbs boneless skinless chicken thighs
  • 1/2 cup yellow mustard
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Slider buns for serving

Directions

  1. Mix mustard, honey, vinegar, and spices into a sauce.
  2. Place chicken thighs in a slow cooker. Pour sauce over top.
  3. Cook on low for 6 hours or high for 3-4 hours.
  4. Shred with two forks. Stir back into the sauce.
  5. Serve on slider buns with coleslaw.

Timing: Start slow cooker by 2:30 PM for 6:30 dinner (on low) or 3:30 PM (on high).

"Monkey Brains" Stuffed Onions

"Monkey brains, though popular in Cantonese cuisine, are not often to be found in Washington, D.C." - Mrs. Peacock

They look like brains and taste like heaven. The kids will both scream and ask for seconds.

Ingredients

  • 6 large yellow onions
  • 1 lb ground sausage or ground beef
  • 1 cup cooked rice
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup bread crumbs
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt, pepper, and paprika to taste
  • 1 cup beef broth

Directions

  1. Cut tops off onions and scoop out centers, leaving 2-3 outer layers as the "shell."
  2. Chop scooped onion centers. Saute with ground meat until browned.
  3. Mix cooked meat with rice, cheese, bread crumbs, egg, and spices.
  4. Stuff mixture into onion shells. Place in a baking dish.
  5. Pour beef broth into the bottom of the dish.
  6. Cover with foil and bake at 375F for 45 minutes.
  7. Remove foil, sprinkle extra cheese on top, bake 10 more minutes.

"Mrs. Peacock's" Blue Cheese Coleslaw

Elegant, tangy, and just a little suspicious

Ingredients

  • 1 bag pre-shredded coleslaw mix (14 oz)
  • 1/3 cup crumbled blue cheese
  • 1/3 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup chopped walnuts (optional)

Directions

  1. Whisk together mayo, vinegar, honey, salt, and pepper.
  2. Toss coleslaw mix with dressing until evenly coated.
  3. Fold in blue cheese crumbles and walnuts.
  4. Refrigerate at least 1 hour before serving.

"Mr. Green's" Corn Salad

Fresh, sweet, and entirely innocent. Or is it?

Ingredients

  • 6 ears fresh corn, kernels cut off (or 4 cups frozen, thawed)
  • 1/2 cup diced red bell pepper
  • 1/4 cup diced red onion
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded and minced (optional)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp lime juice
  • 1/2 tsp cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup crumbled cotija or feta cheese

Directions

  1. If using fresh corn, char kernels in a hot skillet for 3-4 minutes for smoky flavor. Let cool.
  2. Combine corn, bell pepper, red onion, cilantro, and jalapeño in a large bowl.
  3. Whisk together olive oil, lime juice, cumin, salt, and pepper. Pour over salad.
  4. Toss to combine. Top with crumbled cheese. Serve at room temp or chilled.

🍰 The Verdict - Dessert

"Shark Fin Soup" Jello Cups

"This is... uh... what is this?" "Shark fin soup." - Colonel Mustard

Blue Jello "ocean" cups with gummy sharks lurking inside and a gummy fin breaking the surface. The 6-year-old will declare this the best thing ever made.

Ingredients

  • 2 boxes blue Jello (berry blue or berry blast)
  • Gummy sharks (1-2 per cup)
  • Gummy fish or Swedish fish (optional, extra sea life)
  • Cool Whip or whipped cream
  • 12-15 clear plastic cups (5 oz or 9 oz)
  • Optional: crushed graham crackers for "sandy bottom"

Directions

  1. Optional: sprinkle a thin layer of crushed graham crackers in the bottom of each cup for a "sandy ocean floor."
  2. Prepare blue Jello per package directions. Let cool slightly (5 min at room temp).
  3. Pour Jello about halfway into each cup. Refrigerate 45 minutes until partially set (jiggly but holds shape).
  4. Press 1-2 gummy sharks into the partially-set Jello (submerged, swimming position).
  5. Pour remaining Jello to fill cups to 3/4 full. Refrigerate until fully set (2-3 hours or overnight).
  6. Before serving: add a small dollop of Cool Whip on top ("seafoam") and stick one gummy shark fin-up breaking through the surface.

Make ahead: Best made the day before. Add Cool Whip and surface shark just before serving.

"Dog Poop" No-Bake Cookies

They look exactly like what the name suggests. They taste like chocolate-peanut butter perfection. The kids will lose their minds.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 4 tbsp cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter
  • 3 cups quick-cooking oats
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Directions

  1. In a saucepan, combine sugar, milk, butter, and cocoa powder.
  2. Bring to a rolling boil over medium heat, stirring constantly.
  3. Boil for exactly 1 minute, then remove from heat.
  4. Stir in peanut butter, oats, vanilla, and salt until well combined.
  5. Drop by spoonfuls onto wax paper. Shape into... realistic forms for maximum hilarity.
  6. Let set at room temperature (about 30-45 minutes) or refrigerate to speed up.

Make ahead: These are best made 1-2 days before. Store in an airtight container at room temp.
